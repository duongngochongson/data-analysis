SQL eCommerce User Behavior Exploration

The project aims to provide insights on user engagement and revenue trends for data analysts and eCommerce professionals.

Overall

Approach & Platform: Explore a public dataset of Google Analytics in BigQuery.

Main Techniques: Utilize window functions, nested queries (CTEs), and data unnesting techniques.

Result: Extract key insights, such as engagement rates and revenue performance over time.

Query 01 · Total visits, pageviews, transactions (Jan–Mar 2017)

SELECT FORMAT_DATE("%m-%Y", PARSE_DATE("%Y%m%d", date)) AS month,
       SUM(totals.visits) AS number_of_visit,
       SUM(totals.pageviews) AS number_of_pageview,
       SUM(totals.transactions) AS number_of_transaction
FROM `bigquery-public-data.google_analytics_sample.ga_sessions_2017*`
WHERE _table_suffix BETWEEN '0101' AND '0331'
GROUP BY month;

March 2017 had the most transactions, while January 2017 had the highest visits.

Query 02 · Bounce rate per traffic source (Jul 2017)

Google had the highest total visits, while YouTube and Facebook had the highest bounce rates (66.7% and 64.3%).

Query 03 · Revenue by traffic source, weekly & monthly (Jun 2017)

Direct traffic dominated, generating ~$97k in monthly revenue, far ahead of Google ($18.7k) and DFA ($8.8k).

Query 04 · Avg pageviews: purchasers vs non-purchasers (Jun–Jul 2017)

July 2017 had higher average pageviews per purchase compared to June, and slightly higher non-purchase pageviews too.

Query 05 · Avg transactions per purchasing user (Jul 2017)

Purchasing users averaged 4.16 transactions in July 2017.

Query 06 · Avg revenue per session for purchasers (Jul 2017)

Average revenue per session reached $43.86.

Query 07 · Cross-sell from "YouTube Men’s Vintage Henley" (Jul 2017)

Customers who bought the Henley also favored Google Sunglasses (20 units), Women’s Vintage Hero Tee, and SPF-15 Lip Balm — strong interest in casual wear and Google/YouTube branded accessories.

Python · Pandas

Retailer RFM Segmentation

The project aims to analyze the retailer’s customer behavior to enhance marketing strategies for marketing teams.

Overall

Platform: Google Colab. Library: Pandas, NumPy, Seaborn, Matplotlib, Squarify.

Context: SuperStore seeks to segment customers for marketing campaigns using the RFM model — Recency, Frequency, Monetary.

Data Cleaning

  • Drop duplicates.
  • Remove cancelled orders (InvoiceNo starting with C).
  • Drop bad-debt rows where InvoiceNo is not an integer.
  • Keep only Quantity > 0, UnitPrice > 0, non-empty CustomerID.
  • Convert InvoiceDate to datetime, extract Date.

RFM Segmentation Pipeline

  1. Compute revenue = quantity × price.
  2. Aggregate per customer: last date, unique invoices, total revenue.
  3. Recency = days from 31/12/2011 to last purchase.
  4. Score R, F, M 1–5 using pd.qcut quintiles → 3-digit RFM score.
  5. Merge with segmentation lookup to name each segment.

Visualization

Insights & Recommendations

Champions are 19.2% of customers; Hibernating 15.9%. A significant gap exists between strong and weak segments — SuperStore relies too heavily on Champions. Focus on Recency and Frequency over Monetary to convert Potential Loyalists.

Machine Learning · Python

Customer Churn Prediction & Segmentation

Combine RFM segmentation with churn signals to target retention campaigns at the right customers.

Overall

Platform: Google Colab. Library: Pandas, NumPy, Seaborn, Matplotlib, Squarify; scikit-learn for the modeling step.

Context: SuperStore wants to keep loyal customers and re-engage those at risk. Marketing needs a per-customer retention priority score.

Segmentation Foundation (RFM)

  1. Clean transactions — drop cancelled orders, negative quantities, missing IDs.
  2. Compute Recency, Frequency, Monetary per customer.
  3. Score 1–5 with pd.qcut, build 3-digit RFM score.
  4. Map to seven segments: Champions, Loyal, Potential Loyalist, Hibernating, About to Sleep, At Risk, Lost.

From Segmentation to Churn Prediction

Label customers churned vs active (no purchase in N days), engineer features from R, F, M, tenure and category mix, train logistic regression → gradient boosting. Output: churn probability per customer combined with segment to prioritize retention spend.

Visualization

Why It Matters

About-to-Sleep and At-Risk are a sizeable share of the base. Knowing who is at risk lets marketing concentrate budget on customers most likely to respond — not blanket discounts across every segment.
